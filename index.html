About NavSutra

Connecting donors with rural artisans to reduce textile waste and revive traditional craftsmanship.

Our Mission

NavSutra is a research-based initiative creating a circular textile ecosystem that transforms waste into opportunity, connecting urban donors with rural artisans through digital innovation.

Our Philosophy

Circular Economy in Fashion

Reducing textile waste through upcycling and creating sustainable fashion cycles.

Ethical & Community-Based Sustainability

Ensuring fair practices and community benefits at every step of the process.

Digital Empowerment of Artisans

Using technology to connect artisans with materials and markets they wouldn't otherwise access.

Research & Insights

Evidence-based approach to understanding and improving our impact

Motivations for Textile Donation

Our survey of 500 donors revealed that environmental concerns (68%) and desire to support artisans (57%) are the primary motivations for using NavSutra, rather than simple decluttering (42%).

Barriers to Participation

Logistical challenges (pickup scheduling) and lack of awareness about textile recycling options were identified as the main barriers to increased participation in circular fashion initiatives.

Artisan Economic Impact

Interviews with 35 artisans across 6 states show an average 28% increase in household income after 6 months of consistent participation in the NavSutra ecosystem.

Skill Preservation & Development

Artisans report that access to diverse materials through NavSutra has allowed them to experiment with new techniques while preserving traditional crafts that were at risk of being lost.

Textile Waste Diversion

Our platform has diverted over 1,250 kg of textiles from landfills in the first year of operation, with an estimated reduction of 3,750 kg CO2 equivalent emissions.

Water Savings

By upcycling existing textiles rather than producing new fabric, we've saved an estimated 3.75 million liters of water that would have been used in cotton cultivation and textile manufacturing.

Community Development

NavSutra has facilitated the formation of 8 new artisan collectives in rural areas, creating sustainable livelihood opportunities beyond traditional agricultural work.

Women's Empowerment

72% of participating artisans are women, with many reporting increased decision-making power in their households and communities as a result of their economic contributions.
